Detroit MI("The 313")


Founded as a New France fur trading post, it began to expand during the 19th century with British and American settlement around the Great Lakes.

By 1920, based on the booming auto industry and immigration, it became a world-class industrial powerhouse and the fourth-largest city in the United States.

It held that standing through the mid-20th century.


"The Motor City"

Recognized as the historic heart of the American automotive industry, Detroit took on the nickname "Motor City."

The state's automotive industry provided the model for mass production that other industries later adopted.

Henry Ford pioneered the use of the assembly line in manufacturing automobiles.
